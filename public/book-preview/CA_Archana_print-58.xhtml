Procedure for

Offering Bhoga

The procedure for offering foodstuffs (bhoga) is the same throughout the day.

The devotee should consider himself fallen and unworthy, not qualified to offer bhoga to the Lord, but should pray to Gurudev, knowing that he is a worthy servant. One should offer asana to Gurudev and call out to him: “Please offer this bhoga to the Deities you worship.” (We ask the Guru to offer food to Rādhā and Kṛṣṇa, but we do not offer it for him to eat).

Although the guru offers food, the disciple, while chanting the mantras, meditates on being helped by him. The gaura-mula-mantra is always chanted along with the mula-mantra of Śrī Śrī Rādhā Kṛṣṇa, even when the vigraha of Lord Caitanya is not present.

We should offer the bhoga with a feeling that śrī gurudeva himself is offering the bhoga to Śrīman Mahāprabhu and Śrī Śrī Rādhā-Kṛṣṇa.
