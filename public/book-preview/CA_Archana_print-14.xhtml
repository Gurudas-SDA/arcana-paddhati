As it mixes with the sound of churning yoghurt, the vraja-gopīs’ loud singing of sweet and melodious songs that glorify the name, form, qualities and pastimes of lotus-eyed Śrī Kṛṣṇa pervades the entire atmosphere and removes inauspiciousness in all directions.

Take a bath

Then one should brush one’s teeth, wash face and then bathe after passing stool and put on clean clothes. Pujari do not go to the toilet in clean clothes!

Long nails are dirty and contaminated. Therefore, it is a general practice of pūjārīs to keep their fingernails short, to avoid contaminating the bhoga and other articles meant for offering to the Lord. Even during the four months of Cāturmāsya, pūjārīs keep their nails short. Brush their teeth even on fast days.

Mantra for taking bath by mind (mānasa-snāna)

oṁ apavitraḥ pavitro vā

sarvāvasthāṁ gato ’pi vā

yaḥ smaret puṇḍarīkākṣaṁ

sa bāhyābhyantara-śuciḥ

Whether one is contaminated, pure or in any condition or stage of life, if one remembers the lotus-eyed Lord, Puṇḍarīkākṣa, Śrī Kṛṣṇa, one becomes pure both externally and internally. [Garuḍa Purāṇa]

After bathing, comb the hair and tie the śikhā while remembering the brahma-gāyatrī, along with the syllable oṁ.

The śikhā is not braided or kept untied and disheveled. It is preferable to tie it

with a slip knot.

Do not enter the deity room or the kitchen with contaminated cloth or touch the deity paraphernalia and kitchen pots. Do not clean the deity’s paraphernalia or wash the deity’s pots while wearing cloth in which one has defecated or slept the night before.
