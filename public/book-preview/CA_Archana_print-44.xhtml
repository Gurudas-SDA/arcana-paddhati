6) Madhuparka (sweet liquid food)

eṣa madhuparkaḥ klīng gaurāya svāhā śrīng klīng rādhā-kṛṣṇābhyām namaḥ

Offer cups of madhuparka to Their Lordships.

7) Punar-ācamana (mouthwash)

idaṁ punar-ācamanīyaṁ klīng gaurāya svāhā śrīng klīng rādhā-kṛṣṇābhyām namaḥ

Again, offer water for sipping.

Bathing and Dressing

8) Snāna (bath)

Mūrti-śuddhi: carefully remove the Deities’ clothes and ornaments being careful not to scratch or harm Them in any way. Do not drag the clothes across Their faces. Cleanse Their Lordships with a soft cloth or sponge dampened with warm water, wiping off the candana, tulasī leaves, and so on.

idaṁ sugandha-tailam klīng gaurāya svāhā śrīng klīng rādhā-kṛṣṇābhyām namaḥ

Massage Their Lordships with fragrant oil.

idaṁ snānīyam klīng gaurāya svāhā śrīng klīng rādhā-kṛṣṇābhyām namaḥ

Bathe Their Lordships by pouring water from the conch (filling it at least three times with water) while ringing the bell and chanting the Brahma-saṁhitā and other appropriate prayers.

idaṁ aṅga-vastram klīng gaurāya svāhā śrīng klīng rādhā-kṛṣṇābhyām namaḥ

Dry Their Lordships with soft cloths.
